Virtual condition: the worst-case mating size

What it controls

The single number that answers the question "will it actually fit?" Virtual condition is the worst-case envelope of a feature, combining its material-condition size with its geometric tolerance: for an external feature, VC = MMC + position tolerance; for an internal feature, VC = MMC − position tolerance.

This number is what a functional gage is built to. A pin gage cut to the hole's virtual condition either drops into every in-spec part or doesn't — no measurement, no math, no negotiation. The gage encodes the worst case the drawing allows, and the part either passes the boundary or it doesn't.

Look at the diagram

A hole specified at Ø8.0 MMC with a position tolerance of Ø0.2 Ⓜ. The virtual condition is Ø7.8 — the smallest hole that could exist while still meeting the spec, made up of the smallest allowed hole displaced by the largest allowed position error. A pin gage at Ø7.8 must pass through every conforming hole, regardless of how that hole's size and position errors trade against each other.

The right side shows the mating pin's side of the same story: a pin at Ø7.8 MMC with Ø0.2 Ⓜ has a virtual condition of Ø8.0 — exactly the hole's MMC. Pin worst-case (Ø8.0) equals the hole at MMC (Ø8.0) — line-to-line at the MMC boundary. The full floating-fastener guarantee (Y14.5-2018 App. B) is pin VC ≤ hole VC: for this hole, a guaranteed pin would need VC ≤ Ø7.8.

Common mistake

Confusing virtual condition with MMC or with the stated tolerance alone. MMC is just the size; the stated tolerance is just the position. Virtual condition is the boundary — the single number that says whether the mate works under the worst legal combination of size and position. If you're building gages or doing fit calculations, this is the number you need.
