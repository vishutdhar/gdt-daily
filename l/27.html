Position at MMC: where bonuses compound

What it controls

The combined effect of three GD&T mechanics on the same hole: round position zone (lesson 26), MMC bonus (lessons 10–12), and datum features at MMC (the Ⓜ that can appear after a datum letter). When all three are stacked, the allowable position error on a hole can be many times the stated value — but only when the part's actual sizes have given up some material.

This is the single biggest "GD&T saves money" lever in industry. A pattern of bolt holes specified as ⌖ Ø0.1 Ⓜ A B Ⓜ C Ⓜ can yield Ø0.5 or more of allowed position on a real part, simply because the holes and datum features came out a little loose.

Look at the diagram

A worked example: four bolt holes in a pattern, FCF ⌖ Ø0.1 Ⓜ A B Ⓜ C Ⓜ. The math walks through three bonus sources stacked:

- Feature bonus from each hole departing from MMC: up to +0.2 each. - Secondary datum bonus (B at Ⓜ): up to +0.1 from B's departure. - Tertiary datum bonus (C at Ⓜ): up to +0.1 from C's departure.

At worst case (everything at MMC), zone is the stated Ø0.1. At best case (everything at LMC), zone is Ø0.5 — five times the stated tolerance. Two example holes are shown: one tight, one in the bonus zone.

Common mistake

Forgetting that a Ⓜ after a datum letter behaves differently from a Ⓜ after the tolerance value. The first lets the datum feature shift (bonus from the datum's size); the second lets the feature itself grow (bonus from the feature's size). Both can stack on the same FCF, but they're independent: skip either and you leave money on the table when the part legitimately deserves the slack.
