§01 Why GD&T exists 00 / 02
Lesson 01 · §01 · Why GD&T exists In the queue

The limit problem: why +/- isn't enough

Plus/minus on x,y of a hole produces a square tolerance zone; functionally you need a round one. GD&T exists to describe the zone shape, not just the dimension.

Lesson 02 · §01 · Why GD&T exists In the queue

Two languages on one drawing

GD&T does not replace +/-; size tolerances and geometric tolerances coexist. Size controls how big, GD&T controls form/location/orientation.

§02 The Feature Control Frame 00 / 02
Lesson 03 · §02 · The Feature Control Frame In the queue

The Feature Control Frame: anatomy

Five blocks left-to-right: symbol | tolerance | [modifier] | datums (primary/secondary/tertiary). Read it like a sentence.

Lesson 04 · §02 · The Feature Control Frame In the queue

Tolerance zone: diameter vs surface

An empty diameter symbol (Ø) in the tolerance block means cylindrical zone; absence means parallel-plane zone. This one symbol flips the geometry.

§03 Datums 00 / 03
Lesson 05 · §03 · Datums In the queue

Datum vs datum feature

Datum feature = the physical surface on the part. Datum = the perfect theoretical plane/axis derived from contact with that feature. They are NOT the same thing.

Lesson 06 · §03 · Datums In the queue

Picking datum features: function over convenience

Choose datum features that match how the part is mounted, contacted, or assembled. Bad datums = expensive parts that don't fit.

Lesson 07 · §03 · Datums In the queue

Datum simulators

Datums are theoretical; in real life we simulate them with surface plates, gage pins, V-blocks, three-point chucks. The simulator IS the datum during inspection.

§04 Datum Reference Frame 00 / 02
Lesson 08 · §04 · Datum Reference Frame In the queue

The 3-2-1 principle

Three points lock a plane, two lock a line, one locks a point. That's how three datums in priority order remove all six degrees of freedom.

Lesson 09 · §04 · Datum Reference Frame In the queue

Primary, secondary, tertiary order matters

Datum order in the FCF changes the inspection result. ABC and ACB measure differently on real parts.

§05 Material Condition: MMC 00 / 04
Lesson 10 · §05 · Material Condition: MMC In the queue

Material condition: the concept

MMC and LMC let the allowed tolerance grow as the feature size moves toward the looser end. This is bonus tolerance — and it's why GD&T saves manufacturing money.

Lesson 11 · §05 · Material Condition: MMC In the queue

MMC defined (hole vs pin)

MMC = max material on the part. For a hole, that's the SMALLEST diameter. For a pin, that's the LARGEST diameter. Memorize this one.

Lesson 12 · §05 · Material Condition: MMC In the queue

Bonus tolerance: the gift

When the feature departs from MMC toward LMC, you earn that departure as extra positional tolerance, 1:1. Example: hole drilled 0.2mm bigger than MMC → you get 0.2mm more position tolerance.

Lesson 13 · §05 · Material Condition: MMC In the queue

MMC and the gage: virtual condition

MMC + position tolerance = virtual condition. This is the worst-case mating size; build your functional gage to this number and the part either drops in or doesn't.

§06 Material Condition: LMC 00 / 03
Lesson 14 · §06 · Material Condition: LMC In the queue

LMC defined (hole vs pin)

LMC = least material on the part. For a hole, that's the LARGEST diameter. For a pin, that's the SMALLEST diameter. Mirror image of MMC.

Lesson 15 · §06 · Material Condition: LMC In the queue

When LMC matters: wall thickness

Use LMC when you care about minimum material — wall thickness between a hole and an edge, or maintaining enough metal for thread engagement.

Lesson 16 · §06 · Material Condition: LMC In the queue

Bonus tolerance under LMC

Same bonus mechanic as MMC but inverted: departure from LMC toward MMC earns extra tolerance. Less common in practice but identical math.

§07 Material Condition: RFS 00 / 01
Lesson 17 · §07 · Material Condition: RFS In the queue

RFS: the default

Regardless of Feature Size — no bonus tolerance, the stated value applies at every size. This is the default in ASME Y14.5-2018 when no modifier is shown.

§08 Form tolerances 00 / 04
Lesson 18 · §08 · Form tolerances In the queue

Straightness

Controls how straight a line element or axis is. Applied to a surface = line elements; applied to a size dimension = the derived axis.

Lesson 19 · §08 · Form tolerances In the queue

Flatness

Two parallel planes containing the entire surface. No datum needed — it's a self-referencing form control.

Lesson 20 · §08 · Form tolerances In the queue

Circularity (roundness)

Two concentric circles in any cross-section perpendicular to the axis. Slice the cylinder, check the slice.

Lesson 21 · §08 · Form tolerances In the queue

Cylindricity

Two concentric cylinders containing the whole surface — circularity + straightness + taper all at once.

§09 Orientation tolerances 00 / 03
Lesson 22 · §09 · Orientation tolerances In the queue

Perpendicularity

Tolerance zone is two parallel planes (or a cylinder) at 90° to the datum. Requires a datum.

Lesson 23 · §09 · Orientation tolerances In the queue

Parallelism

Tolerance zone is two parallel planes (or a cylinder) parallel to the datum. Requires a datum.

Lesson 24 · §09 · Orientation tolerances In the queue

Angularity

Same as perp/parallel but at a basic (boxed) angle. Basic angle = exact, no +/- tolerance on the angle itself.

§10 Location: Position 00 / 05
Lesson 25 · §10 · Location: Position In the queue

Position: the workhorse

Locates a feature's axis, center plane, or center point relative to datums. The most-used GD&T symbol in industry.

Lesson 26 · §10 · Location: Position In the queue

Position with diameter symbol

Ø in the tolerance block = cylindrical zone, dramatically more permissive than the implied square zone of plus/minus.

Lesson 27 · §10 · Location: Position In the queue

Position at MMC: the bonus chain

Position + MMC + datums at MMC = bonuses compound. This is where GD&T pays its biggest dividends. Walk through with a hole pattern.

Lesson 28 · §10 · Location: Position In the queue

Composite position tolerance

Stacked FCF for one feature: upper line controls the pattern relative to datums; lower line controls feature-to-feature within the pattern. Tighter inner zone.

Lesson 29 · §10 · Location: Position In the queue

Position vs concentricity vs symmetry

Concentricity and symmetry are deprecated in Y14.5-2018 — use position or runout instead. Old drawings still show them.

§11 Profile tolerances 00 / 03
Lesson 30 · §11 · Profile tolerances In the queue

Profile of a line

Tolerance zone is two offset curves following the basic profile, in any cross-section. 2D control on a 3D surface.

Lesson 31 · §11 · Profile tolerances In the queue

Profile of a surface

Two offset surfaces following the basic profile, applied across the whole feature. The Swiss-army-knife symbol — controls form, size, orientation, location all at once.

Lesson 32 · §11 · Profile tolerances In the queue

Unilateral vs bilateral profile

Phantom lines on the drawing indicate which side of the basic profile the zone sits. Default = equal bilateral split.

§12 Runout 00 / 02
Lesson 33 · §12 · Runout In the queue

Circular runout

Surface variation in a single rotation, measured at one cross-section. Catches roundness + axis wobble in one shot.

Lesson 34 · §12 · Runout In the queue

Total runout

Like circular runout but measured along the entire length while rotating. Catches taper and straightness errors circular misses.

§13 Advanced 00 / 04
Lesson 35 · §13 · Advanced In the queue

Projected tolerance zone

Pushes the tolerance zone outside the part — used for threaded holes where the bolt extends through and the bolt axis is what really matters.

Lesson 36 · §13 · Advanced In the queue

Composite vs multi-single-segment FCF

Composite FCF: lower line ignores rotation from secondary/tertiary datums. Multi-single-segment: all datums apply on every line. Subtle and easy to misread.

Lesson 37 · §13 · Advanced In the queue

Customized datum reference frame

Translation/rotation modifiers on a datum letter let you intentionally relax constraint at one datum while keeping it elsewhere. Newer Y14.5-2018 feature.

Lesson 38 · §13 · Advanced In the queue

Reading any drawing: a checklist

Find the title block standard, identify datums, walk every FCF as a sentence, validate against function. End-of-curriculum capstone.
