Lesson 01 · §01 · Why GD&T exists Published

The limit problem: why +/- isn't enough

What it controls

Plus/minus tolerancing on x and y produces a square tolerance zone. Functional fit (a bolt going through a hole, a pin landing in a bushing) needs a round one. GD&T's job is to describe the zone shape, not just the dimensions.

Look at the diagram

The same hole, two languages. Same nominal position, same ±0.10 on each axis — but the round position zone has 57% more area than the square. Both zones reject the same "too far off" parts, but the round zone accepts corner parts that the square wrongly rejects, and it matches how the part actually functions.

Common mistake

"Plus/minus is tighter, so it must be better." Wrong. ±0.10 / ±0.10 is looser at the corners (diagonal = 0.141) than along the axes (0.10), and the corner moves are exactly the ones that hurt functional fit. GD&T position fixes both ends: stricter where it matters, looser where it doesn't.

Lesson 02 · §01 · Why GD&T exists Published

Two languages on one drawing

What it controls

GD&T does not replace +/-. Every drawing has both: size tolerances (how big) and geometric tolerances (form, orientation, location). They work together.

Look at the diagram

The diameter of the hole still uses ±. The position of the hole uses GD&T. Size and location are separate concerns; the drawing says both at once.

Common mistake

Reading the FCF and ignoring the size tolerance — or reading the size tolerance and ignoring the FCF. A part can be in-tolerance on size but out-of-tolerance on position, and vice versa.

Lesson 03 · §02 · The Feature Control Frame Published

Anatomy of the Feature Control Frame

What it controls

Every geometric tolerance lives in a Feature Control Frame (FCF) — a row of boxes that reads like a sentence: "This [symbol] of this feature, by this amount [modifier], relative to these datums."

Look at the diagram

Five blocks, left to right: symbol → tolerance → modifier (optional) → primary datum → secondary datum → tertiary datum.

Common mistake

Reading datums in alphabetical order instead of FCF order. The leftmost datum is the primary regardless of the letter — |A|B|C| and |C|B|A| mean different things on the same part.
